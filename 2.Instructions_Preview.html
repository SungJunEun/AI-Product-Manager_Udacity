Child Pneumonia Identification

Instructions

Overview

Help us determine if the x-ray image is image of healthy or pneumonia child.

Steps

  1. Examine the image.
  2. If the image is normalor pneumonia, check the appropriate box.
    • If the image is pneumonia, check the appropriate box from 1~5 about how much you are certain about the decision.
  3. If the image is uncertain to you, check the unknownbox.

Rules Tips

Tips:

  • Mainly focus on two lungs and diaphragm.

  • Thin web-like vasculature are inside lungs. You could distinguish vasculature and abnormal cloudiness with two properties.
    • Vasculature are structured throughout the lung and abnormal cloudiness are not.
    • Vasculature doesn't obscures the ribs or other organs, but abnormal cloudiness does.

Rules:

  1. Normal
    • No cloudiness inside lungs and ribs are clearly visible.
    • No cloudiness in diaphragm and diaphragm shadow are clearly visible.
  2. Pneumonia
    • Cloudiness inside lungs locates in several or one large area which makes ribs not visible.
    • Cloudiness can obscure the structure of heart, lungs, and diaphragm.
